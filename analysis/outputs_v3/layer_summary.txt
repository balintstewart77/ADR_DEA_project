## Analytical Summary of DEA-Accredited Research Projects in the UK

**Dominant Substantive Domains and Evolution of the Research Landscape**

The research portfolio is concentrated in three substantive domains: Labour Market & Employment (436 projects), Business & Productivity (390), and Education & Skills (294), which together account for the largest shares of activity. Health & Social Care (245) and Poverty, Inequality & Living Standards (183) form a second tier. Over time, the domain composition has shifted notably. Education & Skills rose from 4.5% of projects in 2019 to 35.4% in 2025, while Business & Productivity declined from 45.8% in 2020 to 23.6% in 2025. Labour Market & Employment has remained relatively stable, fluctuating between 27.3% and 38.6%, with its highest share recorded in 2024 (38.6%). Health & Social Care has grown from 14.2% in 2020 to 26.2% in 2025. COVID-19 & Pandemic research surged to 18.8% in 2021 before falling sharply to 0.4% in 2025 and 0.0% in 2026.

**Trends in Data Linkage Complexity**

Across the full portfolio, Single-Dataset projects (537) and Within-Domain Linkage projects (488) are the most common modes, with Cross-Domain Linkage accounting for 245 projects. The trajectory of cross-domain linkage has been uneven rather than uniformly increasing: it rose from 4.5% in 2019 to 19.2% in 2020, fluctuated between 12.4% and 18.2% from 2022 to 2023, then peaked at 30.5% in 2024 before falling back to 20.7% in 2025 and 11.8% in 2026. Within-Domain Linkage declined from 46.5% in 2022 to 27.1% in 2024, its lowest recorded share, before recovering to 45.1% in 2026. Single-Dataset projects have remained the plurality mode in most years, ranging from 38.3% in 2020 to 54.5% in 2019, and standing at 46.9% in 2025. The data therefore shows a notable spike in cross-domain linkage in 2024 but not a sustained linear trend toward greater linkage complexity.

**Main Analytical Purposes and Directional Trends**

Descriptive Monitoring is the most common analytical purpose (441 projects), followed by Policy Evaluation / Impact Analysis (266), Outcome Tracking (261), and Inequality / Disparities Analysis (207). Over time, Descriptive Monitoring has declined from 45.5% in 2019 and 45.0% in 2020 to 27.5% in 2026. Outcome Tracking has moved in the opposite direction, rising from 15.0% in 2020 to 24.7% in 2025 and 23.5% in 2026. Life-Course / Trajectory Analysis, though small in absolute terms (66 projects total), has grown from 0.0% in 2019 to 7.8% in 2026. Service Interaction / Systems Analysis has similarly grown from 0.0% in 2019 to 3.9% in 2026, albeit from a very low base (25 projects total). Policy Evaluation / Impact Analysis has remained relatively stable, fluctuating between 13.6% and 24.2%, with no clear directional trend. Inequality / Disparities Analysis rose sharply from 9.1% in 2019 to 24.4% in 2021, then settled in the 14.8%–17.6% range from 2023 onward.

**Cross-Tabulation Insights: Domain–Linkage and Domain–Purpose Combinations**

The cross-tabulations reveal distinct analytical profiles across domains. Business & Productivity relies overwhelmingly on Within-Domain Linkage (218 of 321 classified projects), with only 32 using Cross-Domain Linkage, and its dominant purpose is Descriptive Monitoring (140) followed by Policy Evaluation / Impact Analysis (107). Education & Skills presents a contrasting pattern: it has the highest count of Cross-Domain Linkage projects (67) and the highest count of Outcome Tracking projects among the top domains (78), alongside 40 Inequality / Disparities Analysis projects and 26 Life-Course / Trajectory Analysis projects — the largest concentration of life-course work in any domain. Health & Social Care accounts for 40 Cross-Domain Linkage projects and is the primary locus of both Risk Prediction / Early Identification (11 of 20 total) and Service Interaction / Systems Analysis (11 of 25 total). Labour Market & Employment is relatively balanced across linkage modes (52 cross-domain, 79 single-dataset, 102 within-domain) and is the second-largest contributor to Inequality / Disparities Analysis (57 projects). Gender, Race & Ethnicity is heavily concentrated in Inequality / Disparities Analysis (18 of 22 classified purpose assignments), with minimal representation in other purpose categories.

**Under-Represented and Declining Areas**

Several domains and purposes have notably low or declining shares. Housing & Planning (50 projects total) has not exceeded 5.9% in any year and stood at 3.3% in 2025. Public Finance & Taxation (45 projects) peaked at 5.3% in 2023 and fell to 1.7% in 2024 before partially recovering to 3.9% in 2026. Crime & Justice (79 projects) has fluctuated without growth, recording 3.8% in 2023 and 7.0% in 2025. Environment & Agriculture (87 projects) has remained between 5.0% and 9.6% across all years. Data Infrastructure & Methodology (40 projects) has never exceeded 4.8% in any single year. On the purpose side, Risk Prediction / Early Identification (20 projects, never above 4.5% in any year) and Service Interaction / Systems Analysis (25 projects) remain the smallest categories. Methodological / Infrastructure Research, at 64 projects total, has fluctuated between 2.9% and 7.8% without establishing a clear growth trajectory. These areas represent persistently small shares of the overall DEA-accredited research portfolio.
