## Analytical Summary of DEA-Accredited Research Projects in the UK (2019–2026)

**Dominant Substantive Domains and Evolution of the Research Landscape**

The research portfolio is concentrated in three substantive domains: Labour Market & Employment (445 projects), Business & Productivity (391), and Education & Skills (285), which together account for the largest shares of the 1,271 classified projects. Health & Social Care (239) and Poverty, Inequality & Living Standards (182) form a second tier. Over time, the domain composition has shifted notably. Business & Productivity's annual share fell from 44.2% in 2020 to 23.6% in 2025, while Education & Skills rose sharply from 9.1% in 2019 to 34.3% in 2025. Health & Social Care has also grown, from 13.3% in 2020 to 25.8% in 2025. Labour Market & Employment has remained relatively stable, fluctuating between 27.3% and 38.8%, with its highest shares recorded in 2023 (38.8%) and 2024 (38.6%). The COVID-19 & Pandemic domain peaked at 19.4% in 2021 and has since declined to 0.7% in 2025 and 0.0% in 2026.

**Trends in Data Linkage Complexity**

Single-Dataset projects are the most common linkage mode overall (571 of 1,271), followed closely by Within-Domain Linkage (510). Cross-Domain Linkage accounts for 134 projects and Multi-Domain Linkage for 55. Over time, Cross-Domain Linkage has increased from 0.0% in 2019 to a peak of 16.5% in 2024, settling at 14.0% in 2025 and 9.8% in 2026. Conversely, Within-Domain Linkage has declined from 47.5% in 2022 to 30.6% in 2025, before recovering partially to 41.2% in 2026. Single-Dataset projects have trended upward, rising from 36.7% in 2020 to 50.2% in 2025. Multi-Domain Linkage has remained a small share throughout, peaking at 8.3% in 2020 and registering 0.0% in 2026. The data therefore shows a growing share of Cross-Domain Linkage in the middle years of the period (2024–2025), alongside a concurrent rise in Single-Dataset work and a decline in Within-Domain Linkage.

**Main Analytical Purposes and Their Trajectories**

Descriptive Monitoring is the most common analytical purpose (472 projects), followed by Policy Evaluation / Impact Analysis (264), Outcome Tracking (233), and Inequality / Disparities Analysis (200). Descriptive Monitoring's annual share has declined from 48.3% in 2020 to 27.5% in 2026. Outcome Tracking has moved in the opposite direction, rising from 13.3% in 2020 to 27.5% in 2026. Policy Evaluation / Impact Analysis has remained broadly stable, fluctuating between 13.6% and 24.2%, with no clear directional trend. Inequality / Disparities Analysis peaked at 24.4% in 2021 and has since settled in the 13.7%–16.8% range. Life-Course / Trajectory Analysis, while small in absolute terms (58 projects total), has grown from 0.0% in 2019 to 5.9% in 2026. Service Interaction / Systems Analysis has also increased modestly, from 0.0% in 2019 to 3.9% in 2026, though it remains a very small category (18 projects total).

**Cross-Tabulation Patterns: Domain–Linkage and Domain–Purpose Combinations**

The cross-tabulation of linkage mode by primary domain reveals distinct patterns. Business & Productivity relies overwhelmingly on Within-Domain Linkage (224 of 322 projects in the cross-tab, or 69.6%), with only 17 Cross-Domain Linkage projects. By contrast, Education & Skills has the highest count of Cross-Domain Linkage projects (31) and the lowest reliance on Within-Domain Linkage (20 of 203). Health & Social Care also shows a relatively high count of Cross-Domain Linkage (33 of 164), alongside a large Single-Dataset share (100). On analytical purpose, Business & Productivity is the domain most oriented toward Policy Evaluation / Impact Analysis (108 of 323 projects in the cross-tab) and Descriptive Monitoring (151), but records zero projects in Risk Prediction, Life-Course / Trajectory Analysis, or Service Interaction / Systems Analysis. Education & Skills is the primary domain for Outcome Tracking (72) and Life-Course / Trajectory Analysis (22). Health & Social Care is the only domain with substantial activity in both Risk Prediction / Early Identification (12 of 18 total) and Service Interaction / Systems Analysis (8 of 18 total). Inequality / Disparities Analysis is most concentrated in Labour Market & Employment (51) and Education & Skills (41).

**Under-Represented Domains and Purposes**

Several domains have notably low or declining shares. Housing & Planning accounts for just 50 projects overall and has not exceeded 4.2% in any year since 2020, when it stood at 4.2%; it was 3.0% in 2025. Public Finance & Taxation totals 42 projects and has fluctuated between 0.0% and 5.4% without a clear growth trajectory. Data Infrastructure & Methodology has 39 projects and has remained between 0.0% and 5.0% annually. Crime & Justice (81 projects) peaked at 9.4% in 2022 but fell to 5.9% in 2026. Gender, Race & Ethnicity (107 projects) has fluctuated considerably, from 1.7% in 2020 to 13.8% in 2021, and stood at 7.0% in 2025. Among analytical purposes, Risk Prediction / Early Identification (18 projects) and Service Interaction / Systems Analysis (18 projects) are the smallest categories, together representing fewer than 3% of all projects. Risk Prediction registered 0.0% in both 2021 and 2023. Methodological / Infrastructure Research, at 64 projects total, has remained between 2.5% and 6.9% throughout the period. These categories represent persistently small shares of the overall research portfolio.
